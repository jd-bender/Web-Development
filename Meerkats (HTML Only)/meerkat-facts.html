Meerkats!

And a few facts about them

Meerkats are small carnivores that belong to the mongoose family. They are located in multiple parts of Africa, and organize themselves into clans. These clans are usually composed of around 20 members, but some can have up to 50 or more! They are burrowing creatures, and live in large underground networks that they only leave during the day.

About Meerkats

  • Kingdom: Animalia
  • Phylum: Chordata
  • Class: Mammalia

Top 3 Facts About Meerkats

  1. Meerkats also share their burrows with the yellow mongoose and ground squirrel
  2. The meerkat is the only member of the mongoose family to lack a bushy tail
  3. A clan always has a sentry on guard to watch for predators while the others scavenge for food
